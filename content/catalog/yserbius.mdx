The Shadow of Yserbius launched in 1992 on The Sierra Network (TSN, later The ImagiNation Network or INN) as one of the earliest commercial graphical MMORPGs. The shared dungeon format with party-based combat and open competition between players supported some of the earliest documented organized PvP guild conflicts in commercial gaming.

The TheMercs guild traces its earliest documented operations back to Yserbius, where the guild's founders Balor and Assassin established the small-elite-roster doctrine that would carry forward into Asheron's Call, World of Warcraft, Warhammer Online, and Darkfall over the next two decades. Notable Yserbius rivals defeated by TheMercs in this era included KOY (Kowards of Yserbius led by Rainstorm), Soul Brigade, KAAOS, DarkSide, and Berserker.

Yserbius sunset in the late 1990s as TSN/INN wound down. The game has not been resurrected on emulator, and the historical record exists primarily through guild memoirs and Sierra Network retrospectives.
