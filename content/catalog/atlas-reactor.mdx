Atlas Reactor launched in 2016 from Trion Worlds with a genuinely novel tactical PvP design: 4v4 simultaneous-turn combat where both teams plan their actions during a 20-second decision phase, then all eight players' moves and abilities resolve at once. The result was a competitive game that played like a heist where everyone is trying to predict what the other side will do.

The format had a real cult following in the 2016 to 2018 window, with tournament play developing organically. The audience never grew large enough to sustain the operating cost, and Trion's broader portfolio struggles led to the November 2019 shutdown alongside parent company Gamigo's restructuring.

Profile preserved as the most-cited example of "esports format that wasn't given enough time." The simultaneous-turn design language has not been seriously attempted by another tier-1 publisher since.
