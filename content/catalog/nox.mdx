Nox launched in 2000 from Westwood Studios with one of the more interesting class-design decisions in any action RPG: the three classes (Warrior, Conjurer, Wizard) had genuinely different mechanics rather than being statistical variants. Warrior is melee with weapon swings. Conjurer summons creatures and uses bow / poison / traps. Wizard is reactive spellcasting that has to be charged.

The PvP arena modes (free for all, team, CTF, king of the realm) were unusually well-designed for a 2000 ARPG. The class asymmetry meant matchups felt distinctive instead of mirror matches. The dedicated PvP servers ran for years.

EA killed Westwood in 2003. Nox was never re-released or modernized officially, but the OpenNox community project keeps the game playable on modern hardware. Catalog entry preserved as a foundational example of class-asymmetric ARPG PvP.
