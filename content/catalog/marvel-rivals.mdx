Marvel Rivals stepped into the hero shooter audience that Overwatch League's collapse left behind. The 6v6 format, the team-up abilities, and the Marvel character pool drew a large initial population. The competitive scene is rebuilding the third-party tournament infrastructure that hero shooters need before any league can stabilize.

The 2024 hero shooter market had two outcomes. Marvel Rivals was one. The other was Concord, the Sony and Firewalk Studios entry that launched in August 2024, hit one of the smallest concurrent player counts of any major Sony title in recent memory, and was taken offline within two weeks with full refunds offered. Firewalk was shut down later that same year. The Concord story became a reference point in the broader discourse about live-service overproduction, the cost of competing in the hero shooter category, and the misalignment between marketing and audience identification. Marvel Rivals succeeded in the same window because it brought IP recognition, a free-to-play entry, and a 6v6 format the audience already understood, where Concord asked players to learn an unfamiliar cast in a paid 5v5 product against an entrenched genre leader.
