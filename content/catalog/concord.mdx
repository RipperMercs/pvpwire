Concord launched in August 2024 to one of the smallest concurrent player counts of any major Sony title in recent memory and was taken offline within two weeks, with refunds offered to all purchasers. Firewalk Studios was shut down later that same year. The game became a reference point in the broader 2024 discourse about live-service overproduction, the cost of competing in the hero shooter category, and the misalignment between marketing and audience identification. The historical significance is the failure, not the game.
