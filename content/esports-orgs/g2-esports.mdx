G2 Esports is one of the most consistent multi-game EU brands in modern esports. Founded in 2014 by former pro Carlos Rodriguez, the org has fielded tier-1 rosters across Counter-Strike, League of Legends, Valorant, Rainbow Six Siege, Rocket League, and Dota 2 across its existence.

The signature wins span genres: back-to-back Six Invitationals in 2018 and 2019 (still the only org to do so), the 2019 League of Legends MSI title in Vietnam, IEM Cologne 2023 in Counter-Strike, and multiple LEC split championships across the LoL franchise era. The Rainbow Six dynasty under Pengu's leadership defined the org's first competitive identity; the LoL operations under Ocelote and later Kold built the second; the modern CS roster reanchored the brand at the top of the EU CS hierarchy.

G2 trades on visibility as much as results: the org has run a content arm alongside the competitive operation since the early days, and the player culture (memes, rivalries, post-match interviews) has been part of the brand identity throughout the modern era.
