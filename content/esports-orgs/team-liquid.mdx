Team Liquid is one of the deepest cross-game rosters in esports. Founded in the Netherlands in 2000 as a StarCraft brand and now headquartered in Utrecht and Los Angeles, the organization fields competitive teams across more verticals than almost any other tier-1 org.

## Active competitive verticals

Counter-Strike 2 anchors the modern brand. Tier-1 CS roster across CS:GO and CS2, with the 2019 Intel Grand Slam Season 2 win as the most-cited team result. League of Legends in the LCS franchise, with multiple split championships across the franchise era. Valorant in VCT Americas. Dota 2, with the 2017 The International title in Seattle as the signature win. Rainbow Six Siege with a longstanding LATAM-anchored roster.

Super Smash Bros Melee: the Hungrybox era is part of esports history; the modern Melee roster continues. Super Smash Bros Ultimate alongside. StarCraft 2 from the org's founding roots. Plus depth rosters across Apex Legends, fighting games, PUBG, FIFA / EA Sports FC, Trackmania, and Quake.

## Chess (March 2024)

Team Liquid Chess launched in March 2024 with Magnus Carlsen as the headline signing, expanding the org into the competitive chess scene. Carlsen's involvement gives the vertical immediate tier-0 individual recognition; the partnership ties the org directly to the Champions Chess Tour and the modern online chess competitive infrastructure.

## Liquipedia

Team Liquid owns Liquipedia, the most-referenced esports data wiki across all major scenes (CS, Dota 2, LoL, StarCraft, Valorant, SSBM, and dozens of others). This is unique to Team Liquid among tier-1 orgs and gives the brand a position in the esports data layer that no other organization holds.

## Identity

Team Liquid's identity is institutional consistency across more competitive scenes than any single rival. The org has been continuously active across three competitive eras (StarCraft-anchored 2000s, MOBA-anchored 2010s, modern multi-game) and has never had the kind of brand discontinuity that has affected several of the other tier-1 NA and EU orgs.
