Fnatic is one of the longest tenured esports organizations in continuous operation. Founded in 2004 in London, the organization has fielded competitive rosters across Counter-Strike, League of Legends, Dota 2, Valorant, Rainbow Six Siege, Rocket League, and most other titles with significant pro infrastructure.

The 2011 League of Legends Season 1 World Championship at DreamHack Summer established Fnatic at the founding of professional LoL and remains the org's most-cited single result. The CS:GO rosters during the mid-2010s competitive peak produced multiple major event top-four finishes, the 2023 VCT Masters Tokyo run gave the org its signature international Valorant win, and the modern Valorant and LoL operations continue the multi-title organizational identity.

The Fnatic brand is one of the most recognizable in EU esports and one of the few orgs with continuous tier-1 presence across all three major competitive eras (CS:GO, post-OWL hero shooter, modern Valorant).
