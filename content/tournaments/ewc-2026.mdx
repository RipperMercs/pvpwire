The Esports World Cup, commonly referred to as EWC, is a multi-game cross-disciplinary tournament held annually in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. The 2026 edition runs July 1 to August 30 across more than 20 games with an aggregate prize pool exceeding $60M, making it the largest single esports event by total prize money in history.

The titles covered include Counter-Strike 2, League of Legends, Dota 2, Valorant, Apex Legends, Rocket League, Street Fighter 6, Tekken 8, Rainbow Six Siege, Call of Duty, EA Sports FC, and many others. The Club Championship aggregates points across titles to award an org-level championship in addition to the per-game competitions.

EWC is funded by the Saudi Public Investment Fund and has drawn significant editorial scrutiny over the political and human-rights context of Saudi sports investment broadly (the same fund finances LIV Golf, multiple top-tier football transfers, and other sports franchises). The competitive question and the political question are separate; both are real. PVPWire covers EWC factually as a calendar event in the esports year.
